**VETTING DOSSIER: K00353.01**  

**SIGNAL**  
K00353.01 is a large (11.6 Earth radii) transiting candidate with a 152-day orbit around its host star.  

**ASSESSMENT**  
The planet hypothesis is strongly supported: the calibrated probability (1.000), high transit SNR (116.3), and negligible centroid offset (0.413σ) rule out most false positives. The odd-even depth consistency (0.3109) is marginal but acceptable for giant planets, and the impact parameter (0.878) suggests a grazing transit, consistent with the measured radius ratio (0.0647). Three transits reinforce the signal’s coherence.  

**FOLLOW-UP**  
High-resolution imaging or radial velocity observations are needed to exclude a faint background eclipsing binary, though the low centroid offset makes this unlikely.
