**Vetting Dossier: K01416.01**  

**SIGNAL**  
K01416.01 is a confirmed but ambiguous candidate with a 2.5-day orbit, a large radius (15.75 Earth radii), and extreme insolation (929.5× Earth).  

**ASSESSMENT**  
The planet hypothesis is plausible but uncertain. The high radius and temperature suggest a gas giant, while the moderate centroid offset (0.2985σ) and odd-even consistency (0.5786) weakly disfavor a background binary. However, the calibrated probability (0.653) and conformal set's "NEEDS REVIEW" label reflect ambiguity: the radius is anomalously large for the period (possible inflation or misestimation), and the impact parameter (0.133) does not fully rule out a grazing binary. The high SNR (171.4) confirms the signal is astrophysical.  

**FOLLOW-UP**  
High-resolution imaging or radial velocity measurements are needed to exclude a nearby eclipsing binary and constrain the true radius.
